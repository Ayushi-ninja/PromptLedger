# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific person is an **Engineering Manager or CTO at a 
Series A SaaS company, 10–50 people, paying $500–$5,000/mo across 3+ AI tools**, 
who signs off on the tools budget but has never audited it line by line. They 
approved Cursor for the team six months ago, added ChatGPT Team, and the bill 
quietly grew. They feel mild guilt about it but haven't had a reason to act.

Secondary user: **Indie founders and solo developers** running lean, spending 
$100–$300/mo on AI tools, who are cost-conscious and love free tools that 
give them an edge.

## What They Search or Scroll Before Wanting This

- "is cursor worth it for small teams"
- "chatgpt vs claude for engineering team"
- "reducing AI tool costs startup"
- "best AI tools for developers 2026"
- Scrolling a Hacker News thread titled "What's your AI stack?"
- Reading a tweet comparing Cursor vs GitHub Copilot pricing

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **Hacker News** — "Ask HN" and "Show HN" threads about AI tools
- **r/SaaS** and **r/startups** — tool recommendation threads
- **r/cursor** and **r/ChatGPT** — active subreddits for each tool
- **Indie Hackers** — specifically the "Tools & Resources" section
- **Slack communities** — Ramen Club, Online Geniuses, YC alumni Slack
- **X/Twitter** — following @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @amasad
- **Linear, Notion, Loom Slack communities** — where technical PMs gather

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1 — Show HN:**
Post "Show HN: I built a free AI spend auditor for dev teams" on Hacker News 
on a Tuesday at 9am EST. This alone can drive 200–500 visitors if the title 
is sharp. The tool needs to work perfectly before this post goes live.

**Week 1 — Twitter thread:**
Write a thread: "I audited our AI tool spend last month. We were wasting $340/mo. 
Here's what I found and a free tool to check yours." End with the link. 
Tag @cursor_ai, @AnthropicAI, @OpenAI in a follow-up reply.

**Week 2 — Reddit posts:**
Post in r/SaaS: "Built a free tool to audit your AI tool spend — found $400/mo 
in savings for our team." Not promotional — lead with the finding, offer the tool.
Same post adapted for r/startups and r/cursor.

**Week 2 — Cold DMs:**
DM 20 founders on X who have tweeted about AI tools in the last 30 days. 
Personalize each one: "Saw your tweet about Cursor — built a free auditor that 
might find savings in your stack. No email required to see results."

**Week 3 — Indie Hackers:**
Post a milestone update: "Launched a free AI spend auditor — here's what I 
learned from the first 50 audits." Include anonymized data on common overspend 
patterns. This drives curiosity and clicks.

**Week 4 — Community Slack:**
Share in Ramen Club and Online Geniuses Slack: "Anyone else feel like AI tool 
bills are getting out of hand? Built this to find out."

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies buying discounted AI credits. 
Every existing Credex customer is a warm lead — they already care about AI 
spend. A personal email from the Credex team to their customer list: "We built 
a free audit tool. Here's your personalized link." converts at dramatically 
higher rates than cold traffic.

## Week-1 Traction If This Works

- 300–500 unique visitors from Show HN
- 80–120 audits completed (25–30% completion rate)
- 20–35 emails captured (25% of completers)
- 3–5 high-savings cases flagged for Credex consultation
- 15–20 shares of the result URL (the viral loop working)
